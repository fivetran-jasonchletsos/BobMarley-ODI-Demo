Dedicated to a best friend

KAMAL
SOLIMAN

Best friend · three decades of road

For Kamal — who has walked this road beside me for over thirty years. Through every chapter of the life we have been writing, you have shown up, stayed up, called back, picked up. You ended up touring with the son of the man whose music gave half of the planet a heartbeat. Of course you did. The right people end up in the right rooms, and the rooms are louder for it. This is everything I could gather of the universe you stand inside of every night — his music, his blood, his band, his road, the line that connects Nine Mile to the next soundcheck. Hold it gently. It's yours.

Person

Robert Nesta Marley

a.k.a. Bob Marley · Tuff Gong · Skipper · Nesta

1945-02-06 — 1981-05-11

Lead vocals, rhythm guitar, songwriter — center

Center of gravity. Born in a hut in Nine Mile to a teenage mother and a white quartermaster who barely figured. Moved to Trench Town at twelve, met Bunny and Peter in a yard, and from there built the first sound that travelled reggae out of Kingston and into the planet's bloodstream. Married Rita at twenty-one, fathered eleven publicly-acknowledged children by seven women, survived an assassination attempt in 1976, headlined Zimbabwe's independence in 1980, and died of melanoma at thirty-six. Sold more than 75 million records on six studio albums; the seventh, Legend, is the best-selling reggae record ever made.
