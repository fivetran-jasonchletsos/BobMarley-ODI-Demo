A life, year by year

Bob's LifeYear by Year

Thirty-six years. 30 life moments and 14 albums, laid out decade by decade. Scroll.

Birth / DeathMusicAlbumAwardFamilySpiritualEventTravel

1940s

1 momentRoots — Nine Mile

1945

Feb 6

Born in Nine Mile

Birth

Robert Nesta Marley is born to Cedella Malcolm, eighteen, and Norval Marley, fifty, in a hut in the hills of Saint Ann.

1950s

2 momentsKingston, Trench Town

1955

Norval Marley dies

Family

Bob's father dies of a heart attack. Bob is ten. He had met him fewer than a dozen times.

1957

Move to Trench Town

Travel

Cedella moves with Bob to government yard housing in West Kingston. He will meet Bunny Wailer and Peter Tosh within a few years.

1960s

8 momentsStudio One, the trio

1962

First single — 'Judge Not'

Music

Bob cuts his first record for Leslie Kong on the Beverley's label. It sells poorly. He returns to welding to make a living.

1963

The Wailing Wailers form

Music

Bob, Bunny, Peter, plus Junior Braithwaite, Beverley Kelso and Cherry Smith sign with Coxsone Dodd at Studio One.

1964

'Simmer Down' tops Jamaican charts

Music

Their first hit. Number one across Jamaica for two months. The Wailers are local stars.

1965

The Wailing Wailers

Album

The trio's debut.

1966

Feb 10

Marries Rita Anderson

Family

Bob marries Rita the day before sailing to Delaware to work with his mother. She is twenty, he is twenty-one.

1966

Apr 21

Haile Selassie visits Jamaica

Spiritual

Rita witnesses Selassie's arrival at Palisadoes Airport and reports seeing stigmata on his palm. The Marley household turns toward Rastafari.

1966

Bob in Delaware

Travel

Works at the Chrysler plant in Newark and as a hotel night porter. Returns to Jamaica eight months later with money to fund his own label.

1967

Wail'N Soul'M founded

Music

Bob and Rita launch their own label out of their Trench Town home. The first declaration of independence from Jamaican producers.

1970s

25 momentsIsland, exile, peace

1970

Sessions with Lee Perry begin

Music

The Wailers fall in with Lee 'Scratch' Perry at Black Ark. Soul Rebels and Soul Revolution follow within eighteen months.

1970

Soul Rebels

Album

First Lee Perry album.

1971

Soul Revolution

Album

Second Perry record.

1971

The Best of The Wailers

Album

Compilation of singles recorded for Leslie Kong.

1972

Signs with Island Records

Music

Chris Blackwell advances four thousand pounds to make a full album. The Wailers fly to London and Kingston to track Catch a Fire.

1973

Apr

Catch a Fire released

Music

First Wailers album on Island. The record that begins reggae's crossover into rock audiences.

1973

Catch a Fire

Album

The first international Wailers album.

1973

Burnin'

Album

Released seven months after Catch a Fire.

1973

Oct

Burnin' released

Music

Includes 'Get Up, Stand Up' and 'I Shot the Sheriff'. Eric Clapton's cover of the latter will hit number one the following year.

1974

Peter and Bunny depart

Music

Tosh and Wailer leave the band over money, touring conditions, and direction. Bob keeps the name; the I-Threes join on backing vocals.

1974

Natty Dread

Album

First record billed as 'Bob Marley & The Wailers'.

1975

Live!

Album

Recorded July 17-18, 1975 at the Lyceum Theatre, London.

1975

Jul

Lyceum, London — Live!

Event

The two-night July run at the Lyceum yields the Live! album. 'No Woman, No Cry' becomes the version the world knows.

1975

Oct

Natty Dread released

Music

First album credited to Bob Marley and the Wailers. Includes 'No Woman, No Cry' and 'Lively Up Yourself'.

1976

Rastaman Vibration

Album

Their first US Top 10 record.

1976

Dec 3

Assassination attempt

Event

Gunmen storm 56 Hope Road two nights before the Smile Jamaica concert. Bob, Rita, and manager Don Taylor are shot. All survive. Bob plays the concert two days later.

1977

Jun

Exodus released

Music

Tracked at Island Studios in London after the attempt. Time magazine later names it Album of the Century.

1977

Exodus

Album

Recorded in London during exile after the December 1976 assassination attempt.

1977

Jul

Toe injury, melanoma diagnosed

Event

Bob injures his toe playing football in Paris. A biopsy reveals acral lentiginous melanoma. He refuses amputation on religious grounds.

1978

Apr 22

One Love Peace Concert

Event

Bob joins the hands of Prime Minister Michael Manley and opposition leader Edward Seaga on stage at National Stadium, Kingston.

1978

Jun

Receives UN Peace Medal of the Third World

Award

Awarded at the United Nations in New York for his contribution to African liberation.

1978

Kaya

Album

Recorded during the same London sessions as Exodus.

1978

Babylon by Bus

Album

Double live album from the Kaya tour.

1979

Survival

Album

Pan-African manifesto.

1979

Oct

Survival released

Music

The most overtly Pan-African of his records. Cover artwork: forty-eight African flags.

1980s

8 momentsAfrica, last songs

1980

Apr 18

Zimbabwe Independence concert

Event

Bob headlines Rufaro Stadium at Robert Mugabe's personal invitation. Pays for the band's flights and equipment himself.

1980

Jun

Uprising released

Music

Includes 'Redemption Song' and 'Could You Be Loved'. The last studio record released in his lifetime.

1980

Uprising

Album

Last album released in Bob's lifetime.

1980

Sep 21

Collapse in Central Park

Event

Bob collapses jogging in New York. Tests reveal the melanoma has spread to his brain, lungs, and liver.

1980

Sep 23

Last concert — Pittsburgh

Event

Bob plays the Stanley Theater in Pittsburgh against doctors' advice. It will be his last show.

1980

Nov

Bavaria — Issels clinic

Travel

Bob travels to Bad Wiessee, Germany for unconventional cancer treatment with Dr. Josef Issels. Stays nearly six months.

1981

Apr

Baptized into the Ethiopian Orthodox Church

Spiritual

Receives baptism as Berhane Selassie ('Light of the Trinity') in Kingston, by Archbishop Yesehaq.

1981

May 11

Passes in Miami

Death

Bob dies at Cedars of Lebanon Hospital in Miami. He is thirty-six. His last words to son Ziggy: 'Money can't buy life.'
